5 Vital Water Safety Rules For Toddlers

Coach Emma Stone
Lead Youth Instructor

Water familiarity is one of the most rewarding skills a toddler can develop, but it must be structured under strict safety guidelines. Unsupervised pools or lack of boundaries present immediate risks.

1. Never Leave a Child Unwatched

Even in shallow kiddie pools or splashing blocks, visual supervision must remain continuous. Avoid scrolling on mobile phones or stepping away, even for a brief second.

2. Establish Pool Boundaries

Teach your child that they are NEVER allowed to enter the pool deck without an adult holding their hand. Boundaries build a clear framework of permission.

3. Lifejackets Over Inflatables

Inflatable floaties or wings can easily slip off or deflate. Rely on US Coast Guard-approved lifejackets when visiting beaches or deep pools.

Nurturing a healthy respect for water at a young age prepares toddlers for standard level progressions and prevents water phobias later in childhood.
